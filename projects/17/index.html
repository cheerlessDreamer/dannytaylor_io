I designed the website for a train delay compensation web app.

  • Brand
  • UX-UI
  • Web

Whilst developing the flight simulator at Black Kite Technology, I worked concurrently on a number of smaller projects. DelayRepay.net was a proof-of-concept service that could be used to identify and claim compensation for delayed train journeys.

Initially tasked with designing the logo, I eventually ended up creating the UX/UI of the mobile website, the full desktop-size website, as well as some merchandise.

A complex database was hidden behind a simple-to-use interface that was designed with clarity in mind. This formed the start of the project - further iterations were rapidly developed as our focus shifted to a mobile platform.

To advertise the website, I designed an Oyster card holder that was handed out at busy stations across London over the course of a few days. Thousands of these Oyster card holders were given out, and membership to the website soared overnight.
